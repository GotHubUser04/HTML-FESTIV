Festivals in the Province of Rizal

The Province of Rizal in the Philippines is rich in culture and tradition. One of the best ways to witness this is through its vibrant festivals. Each town and municipality hosts unique celebrations that highlight local history, religious devotion, artistic expression, and community pride. These events draw locals and tourists alike with colorful parades, traditional dances, local delicacies, and a joyful atmosphere.

Each festival is a unique celebration of the local culture, history, and community spirit. From colorful parades to traditional dances, these festivals showcase the rich heritage of Rizal Province.

Pakaradiaan Festival – Antipolo

A colorful celebration of Maranao and Islamic culture in Antipolo. Features dances, songs, and traditional attire.

Date: February

Location: Antipolo City, Rizal

Kakanin Festival – Binangonan

A tribute to Binangonan’s famous rice delicacies such as bibingka, kutsinta, and sapin-sapin, promoting local culinary arts.

Date: March

Location: Binangonan, Rizal

Tagpos Festival – Baras

Named after a local legend, the festival celebrates resilience and cultural heritage through dances and parades.

Date: March 19

Location: Baras, Rizal

Baluarte Festival – Rodriguez (Montalban)

Commemorates the founding of Rodriguez with military-inspired parades and historical reenactments.

Date: April

Location: Rodriguez, Rizal

Binirayan Festival – San Mateo

Celebrates San Mateo’s rich past with tribal dances, floats, and art contests showcasing local identity.

Date: Varies annually

Location: San Mateo, Rizal

Sumbingtik Festival – Cainta

Named after “suman,” “bibingka,” and “latik,” this festival celebrates Cainta's native delicacies and Christian traditions.

Date: December 1–7

Location: Cainta, Rizal

Binalayan Festival – Teresa

A thanksgiving for nature’s bounty with street dancing, traditional music, and eco-awareness events.

Date: June

Location: Teresa, Rizal

Sapao Festival – Tanay

Celebrates the “sapao” (rice harvest) with rituals and performances that highlight Tanay’s farming traditions.

Date: August

Location: Tanay, Rizal

Aruga Festival – Angono

A cultural tribute to caregiving and Filipino compassion through street art and musical showcases.

Date: September

Location: Angono, Rizal

Salubong Festival – Taytay

Highlights Taytay’s Christian heritage with dawn processions and reenactments of biblical events.

Date: Easter Sunday

Location: Taytay, Rizal

Festivals with Street Dancing

Many Rizal festivals feature lively street dances that showcase cultural pride and community spirit. Here are some examples:

SumBingTik Festival (Cainta)
Features the Caindakan sa Kalsada street dance — a parade of color, music, and devotion.
D'Dalaylay Festival (Jalajala)
Celebrates the feast of St. Michael the Archangel with artistic street dancing displays.
Binalayan Festival (Binangonan)
Includes the Gewang-gewang dance performed while carrying the image of St. Ursula.
Buhayani Festival (Calamba)
Hosts the Buhayani Sayaw Indak, a vibrant street dancing competition.
Higantes Festival (Angono)
Renowned for giant papier-mâché puppets and energetic street dancing.
  • 2. Parades with giant papier-mâché figures and floats

Festivals Featuring Parades and Giant Figures

These festivals highlight colorful floats and giant papier-mâché figures, parading through the streets in grand celebration:

Higantes Festival (Angono)
Famous for its vibrant parade of massive papier-mâché “Higantes” representing local characters.
Binalayan Festival (Binangonan)
Floats and religious processions accompany dance and music along the town streets.
Buhayani Festival (Calamba)
Colorful floats parade while honoring national hero Dr. Jose Rizal’s legacy.

    1. Higantes Festival

    Celebrate the giant puppets of Angono! A vibrant festival full of color and history.

    📅 Date: November 22

    📍 Location: Angono, Rizal

    💡 Did You Know? The giants symbolize the hacienderos mocked by Filipino farmers.

    2. Sumbingtik Festival

    The Sumbingtik Festival is a blend of religious devotion and Cainta’s local pride.

    📅 Date: May 15

    📍 Location: Cainta, Rizal

    💡 Did You Know? Sumbingtik combines “San Roque” and “Kasayahan” into a one-week celebration.

    3. Maytime Festival

    The Antipolo Maytime Festival honors Our Lady of Peace and Good Voyage.

    📅 Date: May 1

    📍 Location: Antipolo City

    💡 Did You Know? It attracts millions of pilgrims every year.

    4. San Ildefonso de Toledo Festival (Tromba Festival)

    This festival in Tanay celebrates the town’s patron saint with river processions.

    📅 Date: March 19

    📍 Location: Tanay, Rizal

    💡 Did You Know? Devotees float religious images down the river.

    5. Binalayan Festival

    A vibrant thanksgiving festival in Pililla featuring parades and cultural dances.

    📅 Date: August 16

    📍 Location: Pililla, Rizal

    💡 Did You Know? "Binalayan" refers to harvesting rituals by early settlers.
